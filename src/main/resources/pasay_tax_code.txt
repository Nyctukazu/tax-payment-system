PASAY CITY BUSINESS PERMIT AND LICENSING OFFICE (BPLO) REGULATORY DATA

[TOPIC: NEW BUSINESS REGISTRATION INITIAL STEPS]
To operate a business legally in Pasay City, an applicant must secure a Business Permit, also popularly known as a Mayor's Permit, from the Pasay City Business Permits and Licensing Office (BPLO). Before applying at Pasay City Hall, single proprietorships must register their trade name with the Department of Trade and Industry (DTI). Corporations and partnerships must secure registration papers from the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).

[TOPIC: PRIMARY REGISTRATION FORMS AND DOCUMENTS]
The fundamental document used to register or renew a business with the city government is the "Unified Business Application Form". There is no document called a Business Tax Return or BTR used for municipal registration. The Unified Business Application Form captures owner demographics, business nature, capitalization parameters, and structural setup.

[TOPIC: TAX ASSESSMENT AND FEES CLEARANCE]
Upon submitting the accomplished Unified Business Application Form to the Pasay City BPLO, the application undergoes evaluation by city assessors. The Pasay City Treasurer's Office computes the necessary local business taxes (LBT), regulatory fees, zoning fees, sanitary fees, and fire inspection charges based on the declared capitalization. The Treasurer's Office then issues an official "Statement of Account" (SOA), which serves as the billing assessment sheet. 

[TOPIC: MAYOR'S PERMIT ISSUANCE AND CLEARANCES]
The taxpayer must pay the full amount indicated on the Statement of Account (SOA) at the City Treasurer's cashiers. Once payment is settled and an Official Receipt is generated, the city will issue the official Business Permit (Mayor's Permit), along with a Business Plate and stickers. The applicant must also complete secondary requirements including a Barangay Clearance from the specific local barangay where the business physically stands, a Zoning Clearance from the City Planning and Development Office, and a Sanitary Permit from the City Health Department.
